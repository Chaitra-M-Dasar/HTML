APJ Abdul Kalam: The People's President

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was an Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He is best known for his work in aerospace engineering and his role in India's missile development program. His inspirational leadership and vision have made him a beloved figure in Indian history.

Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam was a bright student who pursued his dreams of becoming a scientist. His contributions to India's space and missile programs earned him the nickname "Missile Man." He also played a key role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.

Kalam's presidency was marked by his emphasis on youth empowerment and his efforts to promote education and scientific research. After his term as President, he continued to inspire young minds through his speeches and writings until his passing in 2015.
